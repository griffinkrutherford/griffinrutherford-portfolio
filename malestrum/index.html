What a Son Needs From His Dad

Research shows that fathers who spend quality time with their sons can significantly reduce the likelihood of boys becoming anxious, depressed, or aggressive. Boys crave warmth, affection, and tenderness from their fathers, and even non-resident fathers can have a significant positive impact on their sons' development.

When Boomers and Gen Z Collide

Understanding generational differences is key to bridging gaps. Boomers faced challenges like high mortgage rates and workplace discrimination, while Gen Z deals with mental health crises and a slower life trajectory. Both generations have valid perspectives that, when shared, can improve relationships at home and work.

Masculinity and Mental Health

Traditional masculine norms often conflict with seeking help for depression. Men are generally more reluctant to seek mental health support, perceiving it as "incompatible" with masculinity. However, some men benefit from reframing recovery as a "heroic struggle" that aligns with masculine ideals of strength.
